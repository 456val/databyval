SALES PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS

A retail business had sales data spread across multiple time periods, but it was difficult to understand what was driving performance. This project analyzes historical sales data to identify patterns across daily, weekly, monthly, and quarterly periods. It focuses on when customers buy the most, when sales drop, and how revenue changes across different timeframes. The analysis was used to turn raw sales numbers into clear insights that support better planning, improved staffing decisions, and more effective sales strategy.

View Project

REVENUE & CUSTOMER SEGMENTATION ANALYTICS

A retail business across different countries had over 500,000 sales records worth $9.73M in total revenue, but they could not clearly see which customers and products were driving the most sales. The data was messy and hard to understand, so decisions were being made without clear evidence. I built a system using SQL, Python, and Power BI to clean and organize the data and turn it into useful insights. I grouped customers based on how often they buy and how much they spend, studied their buying patterns over time, and identified the most valuable customer groups and products.
This helped the business clearly understand what is driving sales. They can now see their best customers, track buying behavior, and make better decisions to improve sales and keep customers longer.

View Project

PHARMACEUTICAL SALES ANALYTICS DASHBOARD

A pharmaceutical company had 6,185 sales transactions across 970 products but no clear understanding of what was driving sales performance. Product performance, category contribution, and regional trends were not easy to see, making it difficult to plan marketing, manage inventory, and allocate resources effectively. I worked on this data to clean and organize it, then analyzed sales patterns across products, categories, and time periods using Power BI.
The goal was to make the data easy to understand and highlight what is actually driving revenue. This helped reveal key sales trends, top-performing product categories, and important time patterns. With these insights, the business can now make better decisions on marketing, inventory planning, and product focus.

CUSTOMER SENTIMENT ANALYSIS

Airlines receive large volumes of passenger feedback, but it is often unstructured and difficult to analyze at scale. This makes it hard to understand what is driving satisfaction or complaints, especially across service quality, delays, and staff interaction. This project analyzes British Airways passenger reviews using Natural Language Processing (NLP) to classify sentiment, identify recurring themes, and extract key factors affecting customer experience. The dataset includes real customer reviews processed to uncover emotional patterns and service-related insights. The goal is to turn unstructured feedback into clear insights that highlight what improves satisfaction and what causes negative experiences, supporting better service and operational decisions.

Profit Leak Detection System

An FMCG company operating across multiple retail stores in Nigeria was experiencing frequent stockouts, overstocking, and inventory losses, but had no clear visibility into where revenue was being lost or which products and locations were responsible. This project analyzes inventory and sales data to detect hidden profit leaks caused by stockouts, overselling, and expired stock. It identifies high-risk products and stores, quantifies revenue loss, and highlights inefficiencies in the supply chain. The goal is to turn raw inventory data into clear operational insights that support better stock management, reduce losses, and improve overall profitability.

View Project

Demand Forecasting & Planning

A retail business was losing sales because products often ran out of stock or were not restocked on time. This project uses past sales and inventory data to predict future demand using ARIMA and Prophet models. These predictions help decide when and how much to restock products. The goal is to help the business plan better, avoid stockouts, and reduce lost sales by using data instead of guessing.
