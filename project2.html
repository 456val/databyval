Revenue & Customer Segmentation Analytics

Project Overview

This project combines Revenue Analytics, Customer Segmentation, and Machine Learning modeling to provide actionable business insights. It involves data cleaning (handling missing values, outliers, skewed data), RFM-based customer segmentation, and predictive modeling to identify high-value customers.

Purpose

The purpose of this project is to analyze customer transactional data to identify patterns, segment customers based on behavior, and develop predictive models that help optimize marketing strategies, improve customer retention, and increase overall revenue.

Objectives

  • Perform EDA to handle missing values, outliers, and skewed data.
  • Create RFM-based customer segments for profiling.
  • Build ML cluster models to predict high-value customers.
  • Generate insights to improve marketing strategies and customer retention.

Revenue Analytics

  • Total Revenue: $9.73 million across 536,628 transactions
  • Average Order Value: $18.13
  • Top Product Contribution: Top 10 products account for 10% of total revenue
  • Revenue by Region: Highest earnings from the United Kingdom, Netherlands, and EIRE
  • Revenue Over Time: Seasonal peaks and dips highlight promotional impacts
  • Revenue by Transaction Type: One dominant type generated over $10.9 million

Customer Segmentation Analytics

  • Total Customers: 401,548 records with 4,372 unique customers
  • Retention Rate: 69.97%
  • Churn Rate: 30.03%
  • RFM Segmentation:
    • New Customers: 14.63%
    • Behavior Pending: 64.61%
    • Lost Customers: 20.68%
  • Cohort Analysis: Tracks customer engagement over time, revealing drop-off points

ML Modeling Insights

  • High-value customers predicted using RFM features and clustering techniques.
  • Helps prioritize marketing efforts for segments with highest potential revenue.
  • Provides actionable insights for improving customer retention strategies.
