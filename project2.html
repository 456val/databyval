Revenue & Customer Segmentation Analytics

Storytelling case study transforming raw customer data into actionable business insights

Project Overview

Businesses often struggle to understand how customer behavior, purchasing patterns, and retention impact revenue growth. Without clear segmentation and insight into how different customers contribute to revenue, companies miss opportunities to improve marketing, reduce churn, and increase lifetime value.

This case study focuses on uncovering these patterns through customer segmentation, revenue analysis, and behavioral profiling, transforming raw transactional data into clear business insights that support better decision-making.

Methodology

  • Cleaned and prepared 500k+ transactional records using Python and Excel.
  • Analyzed revenue trends over time to uncover peaks, seasonal behavior, and growth patterns.
  • Performed RFM segmentation to classify customers into groups based on activity, frequency, and spending value.
  • Conducted cohort analysis to measure retention, churn, and behavioral shifts across different customer groups.
  • Built clustering models to identify high-value segments and categories with the highest revenue impact.
  • Designed Power BI dashboards to visualize customer groups, retention metrics, and revenue drivers.

Key Insights

Key Insights

💰 Revenue Concentration Risk

The business generated $9.73M from 536,628 transactions, with the top 10 products contributing 10% of total revenue. This shows revenue is highly concentrated in a small product group. Decision: prioritize scaling top-performing products while improving mid-tier products to reduce dependency risk.

📦 Customer Value Structure

Average order value is $18.13, with repeat buyers contributing the largest share of revenue. Decision: focus on increasing repeat purchase frequency rather than only acquiring new customers, since retention drives higher lifetime value.

🏆 Product Performance Impact

A small set of products accounts for a significant portion of revenue, while the majority contribute marginally. Decision: optimize inventory and marketing spend toward high-performing SKUs and reduce overinvestment in low-impact products.

🌍 Regional Revenue Distribution

UK, Netherlands, and EIRE are the top-performing regions driving the majority of revenue. Decision: allocate more marketing and operational focus to these regions while testing expansion strategies in underperforming areas.

🔄 Retention & Churn Signal

Retention rate is 69.97%, while churn is 30.03%. Decision: implement targeted re-engagement strategies for the churn segment to recover lost revenue and improve customer lifetime value.

📊 Customer Segmentation Distribution

64.61% of customers fall into 'Behavior Pending', 14.63% are new customers, and 20.68% are churned. Decision: activate segmentation-driven marketing to convert 'Behavior Pending' users into repeat buyers and reduce churn leakage.

Recommendations

🎯 Strengthen High-Value Segments

Target loyal customers with exclusive offers to maximize retention revenue.

💡 Reduce Churn

Identify at-risk customers early through RFM alerts and re-engagement campaigns.

📆 Optimize Seasonal Opportunities

Use peak-month insights to plan promotions and inventory investment.

📈 Track Customer Value Over Time

Monitor cohorts monthly to detect behavioral shifts and adjust strategies quickly.
