Revenue & Customer Segmentation Analytics

Storytelling case study transforming raw customer data into actionable business insights

Project Overview

Companies often struggle to extract meaningful insights from large volumes of customer transactions. This case study analyzes transactional data to uncover hidden patterns, understand customer behavior, and identify opportunities to maximize revenue. The project examines customer retention, churn, purchasing trends, and revenue streams, transforming raw data into actionable insights that inform marketing strategies and operational decisions.

Methodology

To solve the challenge of messy and complex data, the following approach was used:

  • Performed extensive data cleaning and exploratory data analysis to handle missing values, outliers, and skewed distributions.
  • Conducted revenue analysis over time to identify trends and seasonal patterns.
  • Applied RFM segmentation and cohort analysis to categorize customers by behavior, retention, and value.
  • Built machine learning clustering models to predict high-value customers and segment actionable groups.
  • Visualized results using Power BI dashboards for clear communication of findings.

Outcome & Insights

  • Total Revenue: $9.73 million across 536,628 transactions
  • Average Order Value: $18.13
  • Top Product Contribution: Top 10 products generated 10% of total revenue
  • Revenue by Region: United Kingdom, Netherlands, and EIRE lead revenue contributions
  • Customer Retention Rate: 69.97%, with churn at 30.03%
  • RFM Segmentation: New Customers 14.63%, Behavior Pending 64.61%, Lost Customers 20.68%
  • Cohort Analysis: Highlighted customer drop-off points and engagement trends over time
  • High-value Customers: Identified using clustering and RFM features, guiding marketing prioritization

Recommendations

  • Target high-value customer segments with personalized marketing campaigns to maximize ROI.
  • Focus retention strategies on segments with higher churn risk to improve long-term revenue.
  • Use seasonal insights to plan promotions, inventory, and operational resources effectively.
  • Regularly monitor cohort and RFM metrics to detect behavior changes and adjust strategies.
