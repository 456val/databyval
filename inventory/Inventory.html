Every day, FMCG companies lose millions of naira to hidden inventory issues. This project uncovers where revenue is silently leaking due to stockouts, overselling, overstocking, and expired products ‚Äî and shows how data can guide smarter operational decisions.

Business Context

A mid-sized FMCG company operating multiple retail stores and a central warehouse across Nigeria was experiencing inconsistent product availability, unexplained revenue gaps, and rising inventory losses. While sales data existed, there was no clear visibility into where losses were happening, why they were occurring, or which products and stores required urgent attention.

Objective

The objective of this project was to design a profit leak detection system that identifies and quantifies:

  • Revenue loss caused by stockouts and negative stock (overselling)
  • Overstock and expiry risks at batch and store level
  • Store-level inventory performance and risk concentration

Approach

Structured inventory and sales datasets were loaded directly into Python. The analysis focused on correctness of logic and business interpretation rather than data collection.

  • Computed daily stock levels per product and store
  • Identified zero-stock and negative-stock events
  • Calculated lost units and lost revenue from unmet demand
  • Analyzed overstocked and expired batches to estimate financial risk
  • Built an interactive Streamlit application for decision support
